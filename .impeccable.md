## Design Context

### Users

Friends and Survivor fans who draft contestants and compete against each other across a season. They use the app on draft night (high energy, group setting) and weekly to check scores after episodes air. The job is to make watching Survivor more fun by adding fantasy competition stakes.

### Brand Personality

**Fun, competitive, social.** Playful energy, friendly rivalry, shared group experience. Not corporate or overly serious -- this is about having a good time with friends while watching a TV show.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Visual tone:** Modern and clean, leaning casual rather than formal. Should feel like a well-made hobby app, not enterprise software.
- **Reference:** Sleeper (fantasy sports app) -- modern, dark-themed, mobile-friendly, clear data presentation without feeling sterile.
- **Anti-references:** Overcomplicated ESPN-style dashboards. Generic corporate admin panels. Anything that feels like work.
- **Theme:** Mantine v7 defaults with blue-to-cyan gradient as the primary brand accent. Dark mode supported via light-dark() CSS function.
- **Quality bar:** Fun side project -- works well for friends, prioritize usability and fun over pixel-perfection.

### Design Principles

1. **Draft night energy** -- The interface should feel exciting during live drafts and score reveals, not just functional.
2. **Glanceable scores** -- Scoring data, standings, and player status should be easy to scan quickly after an episode airs.
3. **Friends first** -- Design for small groups of friends competing, not anonymous masses. Names, avatars, and friendly rivalry should be visible.
4. **Don't overthink it** -- Simple beats clever. If a Mantine default works, use it. Avoid premature abstraction or over-designed components.
5. **Mobile matters** -- People check scores on their phones. Responsive layouts and touch targets are non-negotiable.
